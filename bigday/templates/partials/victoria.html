Victoria, British Columbia

Getting There

Victoria is located on the southern tip of Vancouver Island, just north of the U.S.–Canada border and across the water from Seattle.

Guests traveling from the United States may find it most convenient to fly to Victoria International Airport (YYJ) via Seattle (SEA) or Vancouver (YVR), or to take a seaplane flight from downtown Seattle to Victoria’s Inner Harbour. Another popular option is the FRS Clipper passenger ferry, which runs from downtown Seattle directly to downtown Victoria. These sea and air routes avoid driving and offer a scenic approach to the city. Please remember to bring a valid passport and allow time for customs and border processing. Also make sure you are aware of reasons Canada may deny you entry.

Things to Do

Victoria is one of our favorite places in the world and we’re excited to share it with you. It’s a city defined by its natural beauty, rich history, and thoughtful culture. Whether you’re here for a short visit or a longer stay, there’s plenty to explore, but these are some of our favorite ways to spend time in Victoria:

Eat & Drink

  • Bodega – Spanish tapas, incredible vibe, great sherry (we go here every single time)
  • Nubo Japanese Tapas – Asian small plates, LOVE the Goma-ae (we go here every single time)
  • Eva Schnitzelhaus – Fantastic alpine food. Schnitzel, raclette, spaetzle
  • Hank's *A Restaurant – hole-in-the-wall elevated dining, order the tasting menu
  • The Churchill – Like beer? Go here. 50 beers on tap, also has full bar and pub food
  • Tora Tiki – Excellent tiki bar, great ambience
  • Bard & Banker – Historic pub with live music every night. Like stepping into a time portal

Coffee/Tea & Snacks

  • Just Matcha – Serving many kinda of matcha since LONG before the recent trend
  • La Roux Patisserie – Adorable French pastry shop, even featured in a few Hallmark movies
  • Tea at the Empress – Quintessential Victorian tea experience since 1908
  • A&W Poutine – Not a joke, nothing hits quite like a late night poutine
  • Old Tyme Candy Shoppe – Maybe the best candy store you'll ever see. They have everything.

Sightseeing

  • Butchart Gardens – One of the most beautiful places on Earth, with over 100 acres of gardens and a peaceful atmosphere. Shuttle bus leaves from outside the Empress, but requires at least a half-day.
  • Victoria's Chinatown – Oldest Chinatown in Canada and second-oldest in North America
  • Fan Tan Alley – Historic alleyway, narrowest commercial street in North America. Peculiar with a rich history
  • Beacon Hill Park – Gorgeous park to walk through to the coastline, peacocks roam the grounds
  • Whale Watching – Prince of Whales Tours leave from the harbour right outside the Empress

Spa

  • Fairmont Spa at the Empress
  • Heaven The Spa on the Rock, Sapphire Day Spa, and The Spa Magnolia
  • RITUAL Nordic Spa, HAVN Saunas, and Float House Victoria
  • Boathouse Spa and Parkside Spa

Shopping

  • Government Street and the downtown core
  • Market Square and Trounce Alley
  • Chinatown
  • Bay Centre
  • Local chocolate shops, tea shops, and bakeries throughout downtown
