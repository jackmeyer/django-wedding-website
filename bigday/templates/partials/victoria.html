Victoria, British Columbia

Getting There

Victoria is located on the southern tip of Vancouver Island, just north of the U.S.–Canada border and across the water from Seattle.

Guests traveling from the United States may find it most convenient to fly to Victoria International Airport (YYJ) via Seattle (SEA) or Vancouver (YVR), or to take a seaplane flight from downtown Seattle to Victoria’s Inner Harbour. Another popular option is the FRS Clipper passenger ferry, which runs from downtown Seattle directly to downtown Victoria. These sea and air routes avoid driving and offer a scenic approach to the city. Please remember to bring a valid passport and allow time for customs and border processing. Also make sure you are aware of reasons Canada may deny you entry.

Things to Do

Victoria is one of our favorite places in the world. It’s a city we think of often—calm, beautiful, and shaped by the sea and the gardens that surround it. We feel incredibly fortunate to share it with you and hope you’ll have time to slow down, wander, and experience it in your own way.

Eat

Food in Victoria is thoughtful and seasonal, shaped by the surrounding land and water. Many of our favorite places are within walking distance downtown and around the Inner Harbour.

  • Marilena Cafe & Raw Bar – Refined, ingredient-driven dining
  • II Terrazzo and IL Covo Trattoria – Warm, well-loved Italian restaurants
  • Finn’s Seafood Chops Cocktails and Shuck Taylor’s – Local seafood and oysters
  • Brasserie L’Ecole – French-inspired food and wine
  • Bodega – Spanish-style plates meant for sharing
  • Pagliacci’s and Boom + Batten – Comfortable, familiar favorites

Drink

Victoria’s drinking culture is relaxed and welcoming. Historic pubs sit alongside intimate cocktail bars, many in beautifully restored buildings.

  • Citrus & Cane and Humboldt Bar – Thoughtful cocktails
  • Irish Times Pub, Bard & Banker, and Bartholomew’s – Classic pubs
  • Leopold’s Tavern, Superflux Cabana, and Tora Tiki – Casual and playful

Walking Tours

Victoria reveals itself best on foot. Its scale invites wandering, and its history shows up in quiet corners as much as in landmarks.

  • Hidden Victoria Tours
  • Toonie Tours Victoria
  • Off the Eaten Track Food Tours and A Taste of Victoria Food Tours
  • Ghostly Walks
  • Victoria Pedicab

Self-guided favorites include the Inner Harbour, Chinatown and Fan Tan Alley, and Bastion Square.

Butchart Gardens

Butchart Gardens is one of the places that defines Victoria for us. It’s expansive yet peaceful, shaped slowly over time. Wandering the gardens feels meditative, and it’s easy to lose track of the hours.

Spa

Rest feels natural here. Victoria offers spaces designed for quiet, warmth, and restoration.

  • Fairmont Spa at the Empress
  • Heaven The Spa on the Rock, Sapphire Day Spa, and The Spa Magnolia
  • RITUAL Nordic Spa, HAVN Saunas, and Float House Victoria
  • Boathouse Spa and Parkside Spa

Shopping

Shopping in Victoria is best approached without a plan. It’s less about large stores and more about small discoveries.

  • Government Street and the downtown core
  • Market Square and Trounce Alley
  • Chinatown
  • Bay Centre
  • Local chocolate shops, tea shops, and bakeries throughout downtown
